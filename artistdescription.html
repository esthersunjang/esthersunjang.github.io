Things about Rembrandt:
1606-1669. Achieved fame relatively early in life and is known for his paintings and etchings. His paintings varied in size, style, and subject, but they were often highly dramatic (think of Europa being abducted by the god Jupiter disguised as a white bull), and richly detailed (check out the costumes in Belshazzar's Feast).

Interesting things about Rembrandt:
Some scholars believe that Rembrandt suffered from stereoblindness, which causes the brain to switch to one eye for visual tasks and has the effect of flattening images. Stereoblindness may have contributed to Rembrandt's skill as a painter. Art teachers often tell their students to close one eye in order to better view things as they would appear on a 2D canvas.

Despite being well-known throughout his life and earning a high income, Rembrandt eventually went bankrupt as he always lived beyond his means. He also had a series of mistresses, one of whom demanded alimony. Rembrandt had her thrown into an insane asylum for 5 years, but her financial claims against him may have been the final straw in causing him to declare bankruptcy.
