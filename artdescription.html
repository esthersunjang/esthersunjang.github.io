DAILY ART TASTING

Faith and Confidence, by William Beall.
1957.
Photograph. f16, 1/100th of a second.

In 1957, photographer William Beall was in Chinatown (D.C.) for a parade. As 25 year old policeman Maurice Cullinane approached 2 year old Allan Weaver to coax him back onto the curb to avoid sparks from the fireworks, the 2 year old leaned up and asked Maurice if he was a marine. (The child's father was a marine stationed in Japan at the time.) Photographer William Beall turned around just in time to witness the exchange. He snapped the photo and hurried back to the office to develop the film. He realized he had something special as soon as he saw the negative of the film. The rest of the world agreed and Beall was awarded the 1958 Pulitzer prize for photography.

I love the Newseum (News Museum) in D.C. It's one of the few museums that you have to pay for in D.C., but it's totally worth it just to walk through the Pulitzer Prize photograph gallery. This is my favorite photo from the gallery.
