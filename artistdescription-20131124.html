Interesting things about Rodin:
Rodin (1840-1917) applied to the Grand École by submitting clay models three times and was rejected all three times. Rodin was plagued by poverty and had difficulty getting commissions. However, in 1883, Rodin met the 18 year old Camille Claudel, who was also a talented sculptor. She served as Rodin's inspiration for many figures, and also helped him win commissions. Unfortunately, Rodin could not leave Rose Beuret, the mother of his son and Camille eventually left Rodin. Many of Rodin's most famous sculptures (The Thinker, The Kiss, The Prodigal Son) started out as figures for The Gates of Hell, which Rodin worked on for most of his life.
