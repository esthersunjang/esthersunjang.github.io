Interesting things about Rivane Neuenschwander:
Rivane Neuenschwander (b. 1967) is from Bel Horizonte, Brazil. Her art is often labeled "ethereal materialism," whose effects are achieved by using simple, everyday materials to create what the Guggenheim calls "mesmerizing visions." Neuenschwander's art conveys the sense that art is participatory and that the viewer is equally as involved as the artist. This concept is one that is familiar to Brazilian artists as it was established by modern Brazilian artists like Lygia Clark and Hélio Oiticica.

Rivane Neuenschwander may have been born an artist, but it took her a while to find her calling. She initially tried to learn the guitar and flute, then tried to out anthropology, sociology and economics. I'm glad that she found art because I very much enjoy her playful pieces.
